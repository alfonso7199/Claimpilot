CONDITIONS COMMON TO ALL POLICIES - CLAIMS PROCEDURE
Fictitious Insurer Inc. (synthetic document for demo)

Art. 1. Notice of the claim.
1.1 The insured must report the claim within a maximum of 7 days from when they
became aware of it.
1.2 The notice must include, at a minimum: policy number, incident date and a
description of what happened. Without this data, processing cannot begin.

Art. 2. Processing and routes.
2.1 Fast track (direct payment / straight-through): low-amount claims, with clear
coverage and no indications of fraud.
2.2 Adjuster route: medium- or high-amount claims, complex damage or nuanced
coverage.
2.3 Investigation route: claims with indications of fraud or inconsistencies in
the declaration.

Art. 3. Indicators of possible fraud (enhanced review).
3.1 Imprecise or contradictory incident dates.
3.2 Absence of police report, witnesses or documentation in high-amount claims.
3.3 Unusual pressure for a quick payout.
3.4 Request for payment to an account different from the one on file.
3.5 Inconsistencies between the declared damage and the account of the claim.

Art. 4. Payment of the indemnity.
4.1 Payments are made, as a general rule, to the bank account on file in the
policy, unless a verified change is authorized.

Art. 5. Good faith.
5.1 The deliberate provision of false or incomplete data entitles the insurer to
reject the claim in accordance with the Insurance Contract Act.
